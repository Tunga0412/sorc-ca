Temporary Emergency Department Service Disruptions in Alberta: A Retrospective Archive-Based Analysis of Burden, Distribution, and Stated Causes, 2021–2026
Tung A, Nguyen V et al.
Abstract
Background
Temporary emergency department (ED) service disruptions in Alberta are publicly posted by Alberta Health Services (AHS), but their cumulative burden has not been systematically reconstructed. We aimed to quantify ED disruption burden in Alberta using archived public records.
Methods
We conducted a retrospective archive-based descriptive study of AHS temporary service disruption postings from August 1, 2021 to March 31, 2026. ED-related records were identified from two public archive streams: dated snapshot pages of active service disruptions and the AHS news/notice archive. Source records were treated as events before within-source deduplication and as episodes after deduplication of repeated postings referring to the same underlying disruption. Closure intervals were reconstructed from public wording, clipped to the study window, and merged within site-year to estimate unioned ED disruption hours. Notice-derived episodes underwent cross-source reconciliation against the snapshot-derived corpus, with targeted manual review and fixed-interval recovery when supported by the source text.
Results
The archive-sourced analytic dataset contained 1,023 ED episodes after cross-source reconciliation, including 936 snapshot-archive episodes and 87 notice-archive episodes. Of these, 964 were accounted for with existing captured hours, 44 required manual recovery, and 15 were excluded for known reasons. Final estimated burden was 147,456.5 unioned ED disruption hours. Annual burden was 6,289.0 h in the partial 2021 period, 27,951.5 h in 2022, 38,504.3 h in 2023, 33,433.2 h in 2024, 32,547.5 h in 2025, and 8,731.0 h in the partial 2026 period. Across all years, the highest-burden sites were Consort, Hardisty, Boyle, Grimshaw, and Two Hills.
Interpretation
Publicly archived AHS disruption postings can be used to reconstruct province-wide ED disruption burden, but reliable estimation requires cross-source reconciliation, overlap handling, and targeted manual review. The final dataset should be interpreted as a conservative reconstruction of publicly documented ED disruption burden rather than an operational gold standard.

Thrombotic Microangiopathy Following Systemic Adeno-Associated Virus Gene Therapy: A Systematic Review of Clinical Presentation and Management
Tung A, Wolfman D, Acharya S, Villarreal Andrade D, Melchiorre M, Kolski H, Sun HL.
Abstract
Objective
To synthesize patient-level evidence on adeno-associated virus-associated thrombotic microangiopathy (AAV-TMA), describing presentation, timing, investigations, management, outcomes, and supporting an initial approach.
Methods
We conducted a systematic review (PROSPERO CRD420251150401) in accordance with the Cochrane Handbook for Systematic Reviews of Interventions and reported in accordance with the updated PRISMA 2020 statement. On 16 September 2025, searches were conducted in Medline, Embase, Global Health, Scopus, Web of Science, and CINAHL. We included reports of post-AAV patients with TMA-like presentations. Two reviewers independently screened records and extracted study- and patient-level data. Study quality was appraised using the Joanna Briggs Institute checklists.
Results
We identified 656 records and included 12 records reporting 22 patients. Exposures were primarily onasemnogene abeparvovec (17/22) and fordadistrogene movaparvovec (3/22). Symptom onset clustered within the 5 to 10 days post-infusion window. Renal-predominant features were common (among cases with reporting: hypertension 15/17, oliguria/anuria 13/17, proteinuria 15/16, hematuria 9/9). Schistocytes were present in 16/16 cases, and thrombocytopenia was severe (median 17×10³/µL). ADAMTS13 activity was reported in 9/22, and none were severely deficient. Most patients received corticosteroids; PLEX was used in 6/17 cases and C5 inhibitors in 10/21 cases. ICU admission occurred in 8/10 cases, renal replacement therapy in 7/22, and mortality in 2/22.
Conclusion
Post-systemic AAV-TMA onset clustered in a narrow period with prominent renal involvement. These findings support phenotype- and ADAMTS13-informed triage, balancing thrombotic thrombocytopenic purpura (TTP) coverage with consideration of complement-directed therapy in severe presentations.

Burn and Inhalation Injuries Associated With Lithium-Ion Battery Fires in Electric Mobility Devices: A Systematic Review
Tung A, Thornton J, Melchiorre M, Wolfman D, Wong JN, Rosenfield D, Rowe BH.
Abstract
Background
Lithium-ion battery fires involving electric mobility devices are increasingly reported, but their clinical patterns remain poorly synthesised. We reviewed burn and inhalation injuries associated with these events and described incident characteristics, acute management, and outcomes.
Methods
We conducted a systematic review in accordance with the Cochrane Handbook and PRISMA 2020 and registered the protocol in PROSPERO (CRD420261341238). MEDLINE, Embase, Scopus, Web of Science Core Collection, and CINAHL were searched on March 13, 2026. Backward and forward citation searching and a targeted Google Scholar search were also performed. We included human studies reporting extractable original data on injuries associated with lithium-ion battery fires or explosions involving electric mobility devices.
Results
We included 11 records comprising 334 patients or casualties from 7 countries. Most were retrospective case series in burn-focused settings. Severe injuries were dominated by indoor electric micromobility fires, often during charging. Across the main admitted micromobility cohorts (128 patients), inhalation injury occurred in 97/128 (75.8%), operative management in 61/102 (59.8%), and mortality in 12/128 (9.4%). Reported burn severity was substantial, with mean total body surface area values ranging from 14.5% to 27.5% in the major cohorts. In contrast, one paediatric short-circuit series described uniformly minor hand burns managed non-operatively without mortality.
Conclusions
The dominant severe phenotype was an indoor, frequently charging-associated micromobility fire characterised by combined burn and inhalation injury, operative burden, prolonged hospitalisation, and non-trivial mortality. Paediatric short-circuit burns appeared to represent a distinct lower-severity pattern. Prevention should emphasise safer battery design, certified devices and chargers, and avoidance of indoor overnight charging whenever feasible and safe.

Junior Investigator Program

SORC looks forward to welcoming its first cohort of junior investigators in 2027. The program will offer a small number of trainees the opportunity to contribute to a fully scoped, PROSPERO-registered systematic review from inception through to submission, with authorship commensurate with contribution.

Selected junior investigators will take on substantive roles across the full research lifecycle: screening, data extraction, risk of bias assessment, and manuscript preparation. Projects are designed to be appropriately scoped for trainee researchers and are supervised by SORC members alongside domain experts. Participation is governed by our authorship and collaboration standards.

Further details on eligibility, selection, and the application process will be posted here ahead of the 2027 cycle. If you would like to be notified when applications open, contact us at info@sorc.ca.
